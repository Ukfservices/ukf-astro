The UAE–China trade lane is the single most important freight corridor in the Gulf region. Almost everything on the shelves of Dubai's warehouses, retail outlets, and trading companies arrived from China — primarily through Shenzhen and Guangzhou. At the same time, UAE exports to China have grown significantly since the UAE-China CEPA came into force in 2022.

This guide covers both directions: importing from China into the UAE and exporting from the UAE to China. We cover current rates, transit times from Shenzhen and Guangzhou specifically, the CEPA opportunity, seasonal peaks to plan around, and UAE customs requirements at the Jebel Ali end.

2026 Market Conditions: Sea freight rates on the China–UAE lane are currently stable following the disruptions of early 2026. Emergency surcharges that peaked at $1,200–$4,000 per container in March 2026 due to Strait of Hormuz disruptions have eased, and major carriers have resumed normal services. Book 2–3 weeks ahead for FCL to secure preferred sailings, particularly for the pre-Golden Week rush in September.

Your Two Key Chinese Ports

🏭 Shenzhen — Yantian / Shekou / Chiwan

The dominant export port for electronics, technology, manufactured goods, and consumer products from the Pearl River Delta. Multiple terminals — Yantian handles high-volume container throughput, Shekou and Chiwan offer competitive rates with slightly longer cut-offs. Direct weekly services to Jebel Ali operated by COSCO, MSC, Maersk, CMA CGM, and others.

Transit to Jebel Ali: 13–16 days  |  Weekly sailings: 5–7

🚢 Guangzhou — Nansha Port

Key gateway for furniture, building materials, machinery, garments, and general cargo from the Guangdong interior. Nansha is Guangzhou's primary deep-water terminal and has expanded significantly in recent years. Competitive handling rates and strong customs clearance efficiency. Direct services to Jebel Ali available via multiple carriers.

Transit to Jebel Ali: 14–18 days  |  Weekly sailings: 3–5

The UAE–China CEPA Opportunity

UAE–China CEPA — Now in Force

The UAE-China Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement came into force in 2022 and provides preferential import duty rates on hundreds of product categories in both directions. For UAE exporters shipping goods of genuine UAE origin to China, CEPA can significantly reduce Chinese import duties. For UAE importers of Chinese goods, certain categories benefit from reduced UAE duties when the correct CEPA Certificate of Origin is presented. Your Certificate of Origin must specifically reference CEPA to claim the preferential rate — a standard Chamber of Commerce certificate is not sufficient. Speak to your customs broker before shipping to confirm whether your specific HS codes qualify.

What UAE Importers Need to Know (China → UAE)

Importing from China into the UAE is the highest-volume direction on this lane. Here's what to plan for:

Furniture and building materials importers:

Guangzhou / Nansha is the right port for furniture, tiles, sanitary ware, and construction materials from the Guangdong region. Many suppliers in Foshan, Shunde, and Dongguan use Nansha as their nearest deep-water terminal. If your supplier is inland from Guangzhou, confirm the trucking distance to Nansha vs Shekou before booking — the difference in inland haulage cost can offset any rate advantage.

What UAE Exporters Need to Know (UAE → China)

Exporting from the UAE to China requires Chinese import customs clearance at the destination end — handled by your Chinese buyer or their customs broker. Your responsibility as the UAE exporter is to provide accurate documentation.

DocumentNotes for China-Bound Shipments
Commercial InvoiceMust be in English and Chinese, or English with Chinese translation accepted by Chinese customs. Declare full accurate value in USD.

The China–UAE lane has predictable demand peaks that affect both rates and space availability. Plan your bookings around these dates:

LCL consolidation from China:

LCL becomes cost-effective under 10 CBM — above that, a 20ft FCL is worth calculating. The crossover point shifts depending on the week's LCL rates. UKF can calculate both options for your specific shipment volume. One thing LCL importers often miss: add 5–7 days for consolidation at origin and deconsolidation at Jebel Ali — the vessel transit time is just part of the total journey.
