How Jebel Ali Port Works

By UKF Services  ·  6 min read  ·  Updated September 2026

Jebel Ali is the largest port in the Middle East and the ninth largest container port in the world. Almost every ocean freight shipment into or out of the UAE moves through here. Understanding how it operates will help you plan shipments more accurately and avoid the delays that catch first-time shippers off guard.

The three terminals

Terminal 1 (T1) is the original facility, now largely handling feeder services and some regional lines. It is operated by DP World.

Terminal 2 (T2) handles the majority of mainline deep-sea services — the large vessels connecting Jebel Ali to Europe, Asia, and the Americas. This is where most FCL exports and imports move.

Terminal 3 (T3) opened in 2014 and is the most automated of the three. It uses automated stacking cranes and significantly higher crane productivity than older terminals. Major alliances — including MSC's lines — call at T3.

Your freight forwarder will know which terminal handles your specific carrier. For operational purposes, you do not need to manage this — but it matters for gate-in and cut-off coordination.

How a container export moves through Jebel Ali

Booking. Your forwarder secures a slot with the shipping line and receives a booking confirmation with a vessel name, voyage number, and estimated departure date.

Shipping instruction cut-off. Before the cargo cut-off, your forwarder submits the shipping instructions to the line — shipper name, consignee, notify party, description of goods, HS code, and Incoterms. Changes after this cut-off may incur amendment fees.

Gate-in cut-off. The deadline for the container to physically enter the port gate. For most export services at Jebel Ali this is 24–48 hours before vessel departure, but it varies by line and service. Miss this and your container rolls to the next vessel.

VGM (Verified Gross Mass). Under SOLAS regulations, the verified weight of every loaded container must be submitted to the shipping line before the vessel can load it. Your forwarder submits this. Failure to submit results in the container being offloaded.

Customs clearance. The UAE export declaration is filed through Dubai Trade before the container enters the port. Jebel Ali customs will not permit loading without a valid export declaration.

Vessel departure. The container is loaded and the shipping line issues the bill of lading — the document your consignee needs at destination to take delivery of the cargo.

Free zone and Jafza

Jebel Ali Free Zone (Jafza) sits adjacent to the port and is one of the world's largest free trade zones. Companies operating within Jafza can move goods in and out of the port under a simplified customs regime. If your supplier is based in Jafza, the logistics chain for export is typically faster and involves fewer customs touchpoints than a mainland UAE origin.

What causes delays at Jebel Ali

Rolled containers. The most common delay. Caused by missing cut-offs, documentation errors, or the shipping line overbooking a vessel. Always book early on busy trade lanes (Europe, UK) during peak periods.

Customs examination. Dubai Customs randomly selects containers for physical examination. A well-prepared set of documents — accurate HS codes, matching commercial invoice and packing list — reduces the probability of being selected and speeds up examination if it happens.

Demurrage and detention. If a container is not collected at destination within the free time window, the shipping line charges demurrage (at port) and detention (container held outside port). These accumulate quickly — often hundreds of dollars per day. Make sure your consignee is ready to receive.
