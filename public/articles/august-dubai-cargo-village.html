Dubai Cargo Village: The Insider Guide

By UKF Services  ·  7 min read  ·  Updated August 2026  ·  We have operated here since 2008

Dubai Cargo Village (DCV) is the air freight hub attached to Dubai International Airport — one of the busiest cargo gateways in the world. If you are shipping goods by air from Dubai, your cargo will pass through here. Understanding how it works will save you time, money, and the kind of frustration that comes from missing a cut-off you did not know existed.

What Dubai Cargo Village actually is

DCV sits on the south side of Dubai International Airport and is operated by dnata and Dubai Airports. It is a bonded zone — meaning cargo moves in and out under customs supervision, with duties suspended until goods clear for import into the UAE.

The facility covers roughly 630,000 square metres and handles everything from courier parcels to outsized freight. It is separate from the freighter ramp at Terminal 2 and from the DHL and FedEx express hubs, which have their own dedicated facilities nearby.

The main areas you need to know

Export Shed. Where your cargo is dropped off for outbound shipments. Acceptance hours, cut-off times, and the required documentation are all controlled here. Your freight forwarder handles the booking and acceptance process on your behalf.

Import Shed. Where incoming cargo is held after it arrives. Consignees (or their agents) collect from here once customs clearance is complete. Storage charges begin after the free time period — typically 24 to 48 hours depending on the airline.

Special Cargo Facilities. DCV has dedicated facilities for perishables (cold chain), valuables, dangerous goods, and live animals. Each has its own acceptance procedures and cut-off times, which are stricter than general cargo.

Customs Inspection Area. Operated by Dubai Customs. Shipments selected for examination are brought here. Having your documents correct and your HS codes accurate significantly reduces the chance of your cargo being pulled for inspection.

Cut-off times — the most critical thing to understand

Cut-off times at DCV are not flexible. Miss the cut-off and your cargo rolls to the next available flight — which may be 24 hours later, or longer on some routes. The cut-off is the deadline for your cargo to be physically accepted at the export shed with all documentation complete.

Practical note: Airlines set their own cut-offs, which can vary. Always confirm the specific cut-off with your forwarder at the time of booking — not from a general reference. Cut-offs also tighten during peak periods (Ramadan, Chinese New Year, Q4).

The export process step by step

1. Booking. Your forwarder secures space on a specific flight and gets a booking reference from the airline or handling agent.

2. MIRSAL filing. The UAE export customs declaration is filed through the Dubai Trade portal before cargo arrives at DCV. This generates the export permit that the shed needs to accept your goods.

3. Cargo acceptance. Your cargo arrives at the export shed with the airway bill, packing list, and MIRSAL permit. The shed checks dimensions, weight, and documentation. Any discrepancy at this stage causes delays.

4. Build-up and loading. Once accepted, cargo is built into Unit Load Devices (ULDs) — the pallets and containers used in aircraft holds. This happens in the airline's handling area.

5. Departure. The cargo flies. You receive the master airway bill number, which you can use to track the shipment at the destination airport.

What goes wrong — and how to avoid it

Documentation errors at acceptance. The shed will reject cargo if the airway bill details do not match the MIRSAL filing. The shipper name, consignee name, number of pieces, and total weight must match exactly across all documents.

Underestimating chargeable weight. Air freight is charged on whichever is higher — actual weight or volumetric weight (length × width × height ÷ 6000). Shippers regularly underestimate volumetric weight on light, bulky goods and receive a surprise invoice.

Dangerous goods not declared. Many everyday products — lithium batteries, aerosols, certain cleaning chemicals — are classified as dangerous goods under IATA regulations. Undeclared DG shipments are seized and can result in significant fines.

Missing the import free time window. At the destination end, storage charges at the receiving airport begin quickly. Make sure your consignee or their agent is ready to collect as soon as the shipment arrives.

From 18 years at DCV: The single most effective thing you can do to avoid delays is to have all your documents — commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin — prepared and verified before the cargo arrives at the shed. Fixes at the shed are slow; fixes from your office beforehand take minutes.

Getting the most from a DCV-based forwarder

A freight forwarder with a physical presence at Dubai Cargo Village handles problems differently from one operating remotely. When an issue arises — a hold by customs, a documentation query, a last-minute flight change — a DCV-based forwarder can respond immediately, in person. That matters when the difference between resolving something in an hour versus a day is a missed delivery commitment to your customer.
